## CubeCloud Introduction

CubeCloud (also known as 魔方云 or "Magic Cube Cloud") operates under Fengniao Technology and holds memberships with both APNIC (Asia-Pacific Internet Network Information Center) and ARIN (American Internet Number Registry). That's basically the VPS hosting equivalent of having good references.

Here's what makes them interesting: they run their own equipment in their own data centers. No middleman markup, no surprise performance drops because someone else is managing the infrastructure. They handle everything from hardware to network optimization themselves.

Their specialty? CN2 GIA network routes. If those letters don't mean much to you, here's the quick version: it's premium bandwidth that takes the express lane between Asia and North America. Less congestion, lower latency, more predictable performance. They've got locations in Hong Kong and Los Angeles, with both Pro (CN2 GIA) and Lite series depending on your needs and budget.

The company has been around since February 2016, which in the VPS world means they've survived the shake-out period. They support payment through Alipay and accept Chinese Yuan, making them particularly accessible to Asian customers.

**Final Thoughts**

CubeCloud positions itself as a practical choice rather than a flashy one. If you need reliable CN2 GIA connectivity to Asia, native IP addresses, and don't mind a service that's more focused on substance than marketing, they're worth considering. The fact that they own and operate their infrastructure means fewer points of failure and more control over performance.

Their pricing sits in the middle ground - not the absolute cheapest, but reasonable given the network quality. The Pro series costs more but delivers premium routing, while the Lite series offers good value for less demanding use cases.

Whether they're right for you depends on your needs. If mainland China connectivity is a priority, the Hong Kong or Los Angeles Pro series make sense. For general Asia-Pacific hosting on a budget, the Lite series works. For purely Western traffic, you might find better options elsewhere.

The company has been around long enough to prove they're not going anywhere, and user feedback suggests they deliver on their promises. Sometimes that's all you really need.
